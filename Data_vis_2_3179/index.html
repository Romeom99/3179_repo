Deforestation of Brazil

Comparing Brazil to other Countries

Brazil compared to the world, on average, is the largest contributer to deforestation of natural rainforests. Even when comparing to the relative area of rainforests that each country has, Brazil still comes out on top.
Below you can see a chart showing the trend of deforestation of different countries compared to Brazil. Each value has been normalised to the relative area of rainforests of that country. You will notice that when considering Brazil's large forest area, it does not fair that much different to the other top countries in deforestation. Have a closer look at the actual quantity at each point.

Causes of Deforestation

The causes for deforestation in Brazil, stem down to mainly man made deforestation. These types of deforestation come from Commidity driven deforestation, Foresty, Shifting agriculture and Urbanisation. Together these categories make up the vast majority of deforestation in Brazil. There is another major factor as well which is Wildfires, which will be shown in greater lengths further down in this document. The below graph shows the total amount of tree loss (square kilometer) that has occurred in Brazil from each of the causes. Below that, you can create a bar chart for your chosen year ranges.

Wildfires in the Amazon

Wildfires throughout the Amazon, though not as big as a cause when compared to others, is still something to be weary of. In the Amazon there are countless spot fires throughout the year, some years even stemming into the thousands.

Wildfires do not occur naturally in a tropical climate such as Brazil, leading to most of them being started by people, particulary farmers clearing trees, to make more room for their farm.

Below you can see a map of Brazil which will show you the location and number of fires in a certain area. To polulate the map simply select one of the years on the line graph below it. Notice the shear number of fires there are in one year. Zoom in for a better look.

Deforestation between states

There are 27 states in Brazil. Each state has had its fair share of deforestation over the years. No matter the size of the state or the amount of forest area they have, they all have some form of deforestation. Over the years this has been very constistent, with each state having steady rates.
